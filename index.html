Frequently Asked Questions

SEAR prioritizes underwater reconnaissance for mine-risk and hazard detection in routes where traditional systems are too expensive or too slow to deploy.

SEAR uses a dual-mode approach: semi-autonomous mission behavior for coverage efficiency plus human override for critical maneuvering or mission-specific decisions.

Pilot preparation depends on mission scope, but the platform is designed for compact setup, rapid field readiness, and phased validation before broader rollout.

SEAR is intended for maritime security stakeholders including defense units, port authorities, offshore operators, and organizations needing affordable subsea situational awareness.
